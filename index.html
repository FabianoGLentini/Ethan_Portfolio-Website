About Me

Freelance sound designer with a passion for the interactive quality of audio in video games. As a kid, my cousin shared with me his love for electronic music which opened my mind to the conceptual process of “programming” music. Once I had discovered video games as a teen and began looking into how they're made, I began to understand the potential of the interactivity offered and I was hooked.

Since then I have completed a Bachelor of music at the University of Toronto and a Sound Design and Production post-graduate degree at George Brown College. Through those programs and extra-curricular projects I have developed experience with a wide variety of industry tools from game engines (Unreal Engine 4&5, Unity, Source), audio middleware (Wwise, FMOD, Meta Sounds) and audio post-production tools (Reaper, Pro Tools, Ableton Live, Pure Data).

I have had the pleasure to work on several projects addressing various mediums like VR (Teller Falls), PC gaming (Immaculate Drag) and mobile gaming (Space Dojo), as well as collaborating with a small collective of sound designers to make a audio-only game in Unreal Engine 5 using MetaSounds.

In my spare time I enjoy experimenting with technical sound design prototypes and composing music, as well as photography.
